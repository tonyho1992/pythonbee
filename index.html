The Rules

Rules of the Bee

  • Players will first hear the problem statement.
  • Players cannot talk to one another after the problem statement is given except to declare readiness.
  • Players can ask about the problem (constraints, edge cases / etc.) for 1 minute.
  • The instance a Player starts to spell a letter, time will start.
  • Players will alternate, either giving one “command” per turn.
  • Players can use the standard keyboard and the “Clearline” option which will delete the entire line that the two teams are working on.
  • For these specific keywords, use the following phrases:
    • ‘\t’ : “TAB”
    • ‘\n’ : “NEWLINE”
    • ‘clear’ : ‘CLEARLINE’
    • ‘#’ : ‘POUND’
    • ‘[‘ : ‘OPEN SQUARE BRACKET’
    • ‘]’ : ‘CLOSE SQUARE BRACKET’
    • '!' : 'EXCLAMATION MARK'

Functions

  • Must be of form: def fun(....)
  • Watch out! All tabs and newlines must be correct
  • Watch out for runtime errors (divide by zero, interpretation error, etc.)

Teams

  • Each team must have 2 members
  • If you don't have a partner, come to event and we will pair you up!

Time Limit

  • Players have 1 minutes to ask questions about the problem statement
  • Players will have 3 minutes to spell out their solutions to the problem given

Scoring

  • Spelling Bee rules will be used
  • Players who correctly spell out their solution will move to the next round
  • If no Players correctly spell out their solution in a round, all Players from that round will move on
  • When there are only 2 or 3 Players left and only one Player moves to the next round, that Player will be the Champion
  • When there are more than 3 Players left and only one Player moves to the next round, that Player must solve an additional problem to be crowned Champion

Misc and Questions

  • These rules are a general guideline. Finding technicalities is not in good nature of the Bee.
